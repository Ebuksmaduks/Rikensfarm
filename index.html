About Rikens Farm

Rikens Farm is a thriving organic farm located in Rivers State, Nigeria. Our core operations are in livestock production including: poultry; catfish; goat; and snails. We also cultivate a variety of crops including: yam; plantain; sweet potatoes; pepper; and some fruits, vegetables and grains. This diversity of our farm ecosystem allows us to produce our own animal feeds and manure for plants, ensuring the highest quality nutrition, hygiene and soil fertility for our livestocks and crops. We are committed to only eco friendly agriculture, and achieve this through sustainable farming practices such as using recyclable materials at our poultry farm, and adhering to organic farming and less cruel pests and weed control farm practices. At Rikens Farm, we love what we do, and it is our passion for purely organic produce and animal welfare that leverages our contributions to Nigeria’s food security - one family, one protein source, one crop, and one fruit and veg at a time, and that’s the Rikens Farm way.

Our Practices

  • Integrated Organic Farming: We operate a fully organic system where livestock and crop production support each other. Our animals provide manure for soil fertility, while our crops supply natural feed ingredients.
  • Eco-Friendly Livestock Management: Our poultry, catfish, goats, and snails are raised in clean, humane and low-stress environments, using recyclable materials and sustainable housing designs.
  • Sustainable Crop Cultivation: We grow yam, plantain, sweet potatoes, pepper, vegetables, fruits and grains using organic fertilizers, natural pest control, and chemical-free farming methods.
  • Natural Soil Enrichment: We produce our own organic compost and manure to boost soil structure, nutrition and long-term fertility.
  • Environmental Protection: Our practices reduce waste, avoid harmful chemicals, and support soil and water conservation for a greener ecosystem.
  • Contribution to Food Security: Our passion for fresh, organic produce and animal welfare drives our mission to support Nigeria’s food security— one family, one protein source, one fruit and one vegetable at a time.
